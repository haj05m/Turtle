Turtle Categories

Five of the seven species of sea turtles in the world visit the shores of Sri Lanka to breed.
They are the Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas), Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata), Olive Ridley (Lepidochelys olivacea), Loggerhead (Caretta caretta) and Leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea).

The sea turtles are called Kesbawa in Sinhala and Amai in Tamil.


The Green Sea Turtle


In Sri Lankan waters, the green turtle is the most prevalent. A mature female can lay between 120 and 40 eggs at a time, and she can grow to a maximum length of 1m and weigh about 250kg. Because they only have one pair of prefrontal scales instead of the two sets found on other sea turtles, they may be clearly identified from other sea turtles. Small, blunt head with a serrated jaw. Over the course of its life, its diet varies considerably. It eats worms, immature crustaceans, aquatic insects, grasses, and algae when they are between 8 and 10 inches long.

The Leatherback Turtle


The leatherback turtle is the biggest sea turtle and one of the biggest reptiles on earth. It may grow to a length of 4 to 8 feet (1.2 to 2.4 meters), weighs 500 to 2,000 pounds (225 to 900 kg), and measures between 225 and 900 kilograms. The typical adult is between 5 and 6 feet (1.5 and 1.8 meters) tall and weighs 600 to 800 pounds (270 to 360 kilograms). It has existed for more than 150 million years and is the oldest species of sea turtle. They prospered up until the final few decades, when interactions with humans caused significant damage, after the dinosaurs went extinct.


The Olive Ridley Turtle


The most prevalent sea turtle in the world is thought to be the olive ridley. Their heart-shaped shell, which is initially gray but turns olive green as the turtles age, gives them their name.Averaging 2 to 2.5 feet (0.6 to 0.7 meters) in length and 80 to 110 pounds (36 to 49 kilograms) in weight, adult turtles are relatively modest. On Mexico's Pacific coast, the largest of these species can be seen.The Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans' tropical zones are home to the olive ridley. It favors the beaches of Mexico south to Colombia in the Pacific. Eastern India and Sri Lanka have the most of it in the Indian Ocean.


The Loggerhead Turtle

A yellow bottom shell and a somewhat heart-shaped, reddish-brown top shell with five or more pleural scutes are the features of the mature loggerhead. Their belly is a faded yellow, and their flippers are dark brown with white margins. In the Southeast of the United States, loggerheads typically reach lengths of three feet (0.9 meters) and weigh an average of 250 pounds (113 kilograms).


The Hawksbill Turtle


The hawksbill sea turtle has an oval-shaped body and is a small, agile turtle. The reptile's powerful toothless jaws and raptor-like "beak," which gave rise to its common name, give it this physical characteristic. This beak is ideal for biting, tearing, and crushing food. Hawksbill sea turtles are circumtropical, which means they live in tropical oceans, seas, and nearby waters all around the world. Nesting takes place on untouched beaches, from extremely little pocket beaches to high intensity beaches. A typical nesting site would be a sandy beach with wooded vegetation close to the water's edge.










Nesting Period

Based on statistics on turtle bycatch and nesting frequency, six coastal villages from four districts of Sri Lanka were chosen: Kandakuliya, Mattakkuliya, Wedikanda, Kahandamodara, and Rekawa. There have been no reports of turtle nesting or bycatch in Mattakkuliya, a small village close to Colombo, Sri Lanka's main metropolis and former administrative center.
Green turtles and olive ridley birds occasionally stop by the low nesting village of Wedikanda on the island's west coast. On Sri Lanka's southern shore, Kahandamodara is a tiny community with a moderate nesting population. Rekawa, which is situated near Kahandamodara on Sri Lanka's south coast, has a very high nesting frequency. All five turtle species can be found nesting year-round in Rekawa.Kosgoda is located in the southwestern coast and has a high nesting frequency.
